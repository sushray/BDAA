Profile

Energy consumption has been increasing steadily due to globalization and industrialization. Energy policy in Indiana includes "continuing to promote low-cost energy through removing barriers to market entry and creating opportunities for greater consumer choice, diversifying energy resources and commercializing new energy technologies." Decisions by policymakers, such as state and local governments, utilities and regulatory agencies, affect all citizens economically and environmentally, and are generally geared toward providing reliable, affordable energy. The cost of energy affects not only home heating and electricity bills, and thus disposable income, but also economic growth, including jobs, investment and the cost of doing business in the state. Energy policy directly affects the environment, so there is often significant overlap between energy and environmental policy.

State Facts

In Indiana

  • residents spent $4.669 million on energy.
  • the population was 6.6 million.
  • residents consumed 2.786 trillion BTU of energy.
  • per capita income was $38,812.

Indiana

  • is a net electricity exporter.
  • has renewable energy in the form of hydropower and wind,
    as well as some geothermal energy.
  • residents drove 78,923 million miles, almost 11,958 miles per resident.
